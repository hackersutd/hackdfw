Dallas's first major student hackathon
February 28 - March 1, 2015

Information Results

HackDFW is Dallas's first major student hackathon.

Hosted by students at UTD, SMU, and UNT, HackDFW brings all of the Dallas area schools together in one exciting 24-hour event.

FAQ

Making a trip to Dallas?

The gates will open on Saturday, February 28th at 10:00am and continue until Sunday, March 1st. HackDFW ends by 5:00pm.

Who can attend?

All students are welcome! This includes high schoolers, undergraduate students, and graduate students!

How big are teams?

Teams are composed of 2-5 people. You can do it alone, but it'll be less fun!

What if I don't have teammates?

We'll have a time scheduled where people can form teams.

How much does this event cost?

HackDFW is 100% free! This includes WiFi, food, drinks, and miscellaneous swag for the entire hackathon!

What should I bring?

Laptops, chargers, cellphones, and of course passion are helpful. Toiletries and a change of clothes are also useful. Knives, firearms, and other dangerous things are not permitted at the event.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build something new within a set amount of time. Therefore, you cannot work on past projects. Using your own open-source or third party libraries is fine as long as the project is new.

What kind of hardware will you have?

Our friends will be providing Oculus Rifts, Myo arm-bands, Leap Motions, and Arduinos to hack on for the duration of the event.

Will there be buses to HackDFW?

Yes! We have buses from Austin, College Station, Houston, and select DFW schools!

Will HackDFW provide reimbursements?

We are working with our budget to get a select set of applicants reimbursement to the event. We will be contacting some of you after HackDFW is over.

Where will it be?

We are hosting HackDFW at The Women's Museum in Dallas.

I don't see a registration option?

HackDFW has reached capacity and has closed registration for HackDFW 2015. We had over 1600 students apply to attend this year, a lot more than expected! HackDFW will be an annual event and we hope to see you next year!
